TOP MOVIES OF THE MONTH

DIVERGENT (2014)  |  Rated: PG-13

In a futuristic post war, torn Chicago, society is divided into five groups based on personal traits: Abnegation, Dauntless, Erudite, Candor, and Amity. At the age of sixteen, the youth must decide to stay with his/her family fraction or integrate into another fraction. Beatrice Prior (Shailene Woodley) must decide to stay with her family fraction (Abnegation) or pick another fraction. Prior has been warned that she's a “Divergent” – one who doesn't fit any one group. Collectively, the fraction's view – Detergents as dangerous since they do not conform to just one fraction and are capable of free-thinking.

Before Beatrice can be accepted into another group, she has to participate in a highly competitive initiation process. During the process, Beatrice changes her name to Tris to signal a new beginning. Tris must make a tough decision while she deals with friends, initiations, and a love interest. Tris must also keep a secret from everyone that could lead to her death, if revealed.

Stars: Shailene Woodley, Theo James, Kate Winslet
Writers: Evan Daugherty (screenplay), Vanessa Taylor (screenplay),
Veronica Roth (novel)
Director: Neil Burger, Genre: Drama


THE LEGO MOVIE (2014)  |  Rated: PG

Based on the popular building toy LEGO, the LEGO Movie is about an ordinary LEGO character, Emmet who has been mistaken as a "Master Builder" and the key to circumventing the local bad guy, Lord Business, from rearranging the LEGO universe.

Although Emmet's friends find out that he's just an "ordinary person," they still want him to "lead the charge" to stop Lord Business. Emmet and his friends take off on an epic adventure that's harrowing, insightful, and entertaining to bring Lord Business to justice and save the LEGO universe.

Stars: Will Arnett, Elizabeth Banks, Alison Brie
Writers: Dan Hageman (story), Kevin Hageman (story),
Phil Lord (story and screenplay), Christopher Miller (story and screenplay),
Director: Phil Lord, Christopher Miller
Genre: Comedy


BREATHE IN (2013)  |  Rated: R

Keith (Guy Pearce), a married, disheartened music teacher who wishes for his carefree creative youth. Then, young beautiful British exchange student Sophie (Felicity Jones) comes to visit Keith's daughter. Sophie's beauty and musical talent make Keith uncomfortable. During this time, Keith's wife, Megan (Amy Ryan), and daughter, Lauren (Mackenzie Davis), focuses on Lauren's senior year of high school. Meanwhile, Sophie and Keith have become soulmates due to their need for artistic expression.

Guy Pearce does an excellent job playing the - me against the world - keith character. Conversely, Sophie conveys the inward-looking and ignorant insolence of a brat who's used to getting her way. The film is very controlled and well-executed in which the passionate climax between Keith and Sophie never quite happens.

Stars: Guy Pearce, Felicity Jones, Amy Ryan, Mackenzie Davis
Writers: Drake Doremus, Ben York Jones
Director: Drake Doremus
Genre: Drama
